Hiking Guidelines

What to Wear

  • Comfortable hiking boots or trail shoes
  • Moisture-wicking clothing
  • Weather-appropriate layers
  • Hat and sunglasses
  • Rain gear (check forecast)

What to Bring

  • Backpack (15-20L)
  • Water (min. 1.5 liters)
  • Snacks and lunch
  • Navigation tools (map, compass, GPS)
  • First aid kit
  • Mobile phone (fully charged)

Safety Tips

  • Check weather forecast before departure
  • Inform someone about your route and ETA
  • Stay on marked trails
  • Respect wildlife and nature
  • Carry emergency contacts
  • Start early to finish before dark

Environmental Ethics

  • Leave no trace - pack out all trash
  • Stay on designated trails
  • Respect wildlife - observe from distance
  • No picking plants or disturbing habitats
  • Use biodegradable products

Emergency Information

Emergency Contacts

First Aid Tips

  • For minor injuries, use your first aid kit
  • Stay calm and assess the situation
  • If serious, call emergency services (112)
  • Share your exact location (use map coordinates)
  • If lost, stay where you are and call for help

Frequently Asked Questions

How difficult is this hiking trail?

+

The Teufelsberg trail is considered moderate difficulty. With a total distance of 10.8 km and an elevation gain of 140 m, it's suitable for hikers with basic experience and reasonable fitness levels.

How long does it take to complete the hike?

+

The average completion time is approximately 2 hours and 56 minutes at a pace of 3.7 km/h. However, this can vary depending on your hiking speed, rest breaks, and time spent enjoying viewpoints.

Is the trail well-marked?

+

Yes, the trail is generally well-marked with signs and waypoints. However, we recommend using this interactive guide and possibly downloading the offline map for additional navigation security.

Are there water sources along the trail?

+

There are limited water sources along the trail. It's recommended to bring at least 1.5 liters of water per person, especially during warmer months.

Is the trail suitable for children?

+

The trail can be suitable for children with hiking experience, but consider the full length (10.8 km) might be challenging for younger children. There are some shorter segments that could be more appropriate for families with kids.

What is the best time of year to hike this trail?

+

The trail is accessible year-round, but the most pleasant conditions are typically from late spring to early autumn (May to October). Winter hiking is possible but requires appropriate gear and caution for potentially icy conditions.

Are dogs allowed on the trail?

+

Yes, dogs are generally allowed but should be kept on a leash to protect wildlife and other hikers. Remember to bring water for your dog and bags to clean up after them.
